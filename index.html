How to Play Scoundrel

Set-up and goal:

Scoundrel is played with a standard 52 card deck with the all red Aces, Kings, Queens, and Jacks removed (end result of a 44 card deck).

Your health starts at 20. It can never go higher than 20. If it ever gets to 0, you lose.

The top four cards are placed in front of you. These are collectively known as a "room", which you will have to clear (described more in the rules section).

To win, get through the entire deck and clear out the final room entirely.

Rules

The 26 black cards (clubs and spades) are monsters. They deal damage equal to their value (2-10, Jack is 11, Queen is 12, King is 13, and Ace is 14).

The 9 diamonds are weapons. These block an amount of monster damage equal to their value once equiped. Equiped diamonds are placed below the four room cards. Once picked up, diamonds MUST be equiped, even if you have to discard your currently equiped daimond to do so.

The 9 hearts are health potions, restoring health equal to their value. You can only use one health potion per turn, if there are more in the room, they must be discarded instead. You can never go above 20 health.

Gameplay

At the start of each turn, flip cards off the top of the deck until there are four infront of you. This is a "room" in the dungeon.

You can flee from a room if you haven't interacted with it yet. If you do, those four cards are placed on the bottom of the deck, aka, the dungeon. You can flee as much as you want, but never twice in a row.

If you decide to face a room, you need to clear 3 of the 4 cards it contains in order to move onto the next turn/room.

If you chose a diamond, you MUST equip it (even if doing so discards your currently equiped weapon).

If you chose a heart, dragging it to your health, you heal for that much. You can only heal ONCE per turn. Other hearts need to be discarded if not kept as the last card of the four card room.

If you chose a clubs or spades, you fight the monster. If you drag it to your health (or have no weapon equiped) you will fight it unarmed, taking the full damage. It is then discarded. If you fight a monster with your weapon by dragging it onto your weapon, it will deal damage equal to it's power - your weapon's power. NOTE: in this case, keep the monster on top of the weapon. Once used on a monster, a weapon can only be used against a monster with LESS power than the one most recently defeated.

Website Tips

THERE IS NO UNDO BUTTON - take care before you make any moves.

Hovering over the deck will show you how many cards are left in it.

When dragging a card, suitable places to drop it will highlight as you pass over them. Green will heal you, gold will equip the card, dark red will fight a monster, and bright red will discard.
